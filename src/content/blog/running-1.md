If you aren't one of the few people that know how I tend to bounce around hobbies every couple months or so trying to find things that click with me, then here's my current hobby: running. It's an extremely humbling sport.

I haven't done this type of extensive conditioning since high school, and truth be told, I didn't really put that much effort into it back then. And as the stubborn and overly competitive person I am, I've convinced myself that I can run a marathon in the **_hopefully_** not so far future.

Obviously I don't expect myself to run a marathon first thing tomorrow morning, so I've been slowly incorporating running into my lifestyle. Routine runs every other day or say, with the goal of increasing volume or intensity with each run. I'll start by training for a 5k, and then a 10k, and then a half marathon, and then I'll tackle an actual marathon. I don't want to rush myself, but I'm eager to see progress.

It's been two weeks since my first run, and I've only experienced frustration. Whether its shin splints or my inability to maintain a low heart rate even at the slowest of paces, running has really been a pain. But, as I've mentioned previously, I am stubborn. So you best believe I'm going to keep trying.

I plan on writing about my running adventures occasionally, but for those of you who would like to see more of training specifics, feel free to follow me on [Strava](https://www.strava.com/athletes/115607843).
