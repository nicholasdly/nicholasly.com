Just before I felt like giving up due to my frustrations with shin splints, I left the country for two weeks with my sister for a responsibility free getaway to Amsterdam and London. The vacation was definitely appreciated, both mentally and physically, since it allowed my body to rest and fully recover from the sudden milage I was putting on. I also bought a pair of max cushion shoes to assist with my vacation recovery and for the improved confidence that my shoes weren't the cause of my injuries.

When I got back home, I was eager to start running again as my shin splints were completely gone. I'm not entirely sure if it was the shoes or the rest--it was probably both--but I was excited nonetheless.

On the other hand, I had done a lot of online reading about running while I was on vacation, and I discovered I was making quite the mistake in my training. I wasn't following a plan, and instead was just winging my runs. That proved to be an issue, as I wasn't _progressively_ increasing the volume or intensity of runs. I was simply just running for as long as I could on random days. To solve this, I started using [Runna](https://www.runna.com/), an app with running plans and online coaching. And off I went to follow a simple "New To Running Plan," where I had to learn to leave my competitive pride behind and start things slow.

I am so incredibly grateful I did that.

Now approaching two months of semi-consistent running, I haven't experienced any injuries following my running plan, and the progress has been slow but steady. I've noticed I can run comfortably for longer, and I'm actually starting to enjoy the outdoors as I run. Who knew following a plan and a coach would make you better at something? Funny how that works.

I have four weeks left on my eight week introductory running plan, before I end it by racing a 5k at a local [parkrun](https://www.parkrun.us/). Reading my first post on running, I laugh at myself for being so naive. I'm looking forward to finishing my running plan, and I can't wait to see how far I'll go.

If you're interested in seeing the details of my training, feel free to follow me on [Strava](https://www.strava.com/athletes/115607843).
