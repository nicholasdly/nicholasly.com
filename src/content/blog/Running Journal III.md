It's been just over a month since my last update, and I am happy to share I have lots of good news! I've finished all my 5K training by following the "New to Running Plan" on [Runna](https://www.runna.com/), and concluded the first chapter of my running journey by running the best 5K I have ever run in my life.

I've never been able to run a full 5K before, and I've tried twice for charity events. I've always stopped to walk due to side cramps, nausea, or just straightforward exhaustion. After following my training plan, I was able to finish the [Howard Temin Lakeshore Path parkrun](https://www.parkrun.us/howardteminlakeshorepath/) with an incredible personal record of **25:56**, cutting over **_ten minutes_** from my previous 5K time. I did not experience cramps or nausea, and instead felt amazing—but equally as tired—throughout my run. I was on cloud nine after that performance. Now I just want to run more.

With that said, I'd also like to talk about my first [parkrun](https://www.parkrun.us/) experience. It was wonderful, as both the volunteers and athletes were so welcoming and nice to talk to. Everyone was kind and supportive, and it was so cool to talk to more experienced parkrunners or even foreign parkrunners. I would highly recommend everyone to attend a parkrun if possible, and the fact that it is completely free is just icing on the cake.

After my race, I took a bit over a week off. I had already dealt with injuries like shin splints in the past, and I didn't want to risk it. I'm happy to say that my recovery break is over though, and I have recently started a 10K training plan. With over double the weekly training miles and winter coming right around the corner, I'll admit I'm intimidated, but I won't let that stop me from chasing the satisfaction of racing.

I look forward to the next ten weeks of training, as I've already smashed my own expectations out of the park. If you're interested in seeing the details of my training, feel free to follow me on [Strava](https://www.strava.com/athletes/115607843).
